My Journey

My dad taught me how to play chess when I was in middle school, I first saw the game of chess when I even younger than that, but it wasn't until middle school when I was really interested.

Learning

Chess pieces

Pawn Rook Knight Bishop Queen King

Chess pieces have different movements so lets take a look them.

Pawn

This is the Pawn piece, this piece offers protection to the other valuable pieces and also added benefit of a promotion if you can get it to the enemies backline. Where it turns into a queen.

Rook

The Rook piece is a excellent way to trap the king with its linear movement.

Knight

The knight moves in what my dad explained it to me as an L shape. When I first started playing chess I didn't know what use a knight was, but after a few games I realised the knight can be used against defensive players as after a few L's you could position yourself into dangerous positions without endangering your own position. Only problem is your opposition also has two.

Bishop

The Bishop moves in diagonal Movements, I believe the Knight and Bishop work well together as the you can use them without much disturbance to the Pawn line. If you want to maintain a good defensive stragety.

Queen

The queen is the most versatile piece on the chessboard and it can move in any direction, this enables it to be the best at protecting your king or attacking the opposition king. Attacking the king with the queen is a risky strategy as you may lose your queen in the process. Normally to a knight in my experience.

King

The king is the root objective of the game, and that objective is to capture the king. whereas the queen can manouerve multiple squares in any direction, the king can only move one square at a time, so his protection needs to come from the only pieces.

The Objective of Chess

Now that you Know the pieces associated with the game of chess lets discuss the objective of the game.

The Objective of the game is to capture the king while protecting your own king.

There are many different strageties for chess and I believe that the experience of playing numberous different people is the best way to gain strategies for yourself.

You could also look online further for tips to improve your game, but every game is not the same, so learning to adapt to the opposition player quickly is the way to go.

I am not a professional so my experience of playing is limited.
